🫀 Thorax Anatomy Rapid Review

Big Picture & Boundaries

Thoracic Cavity at a Glance HY

▾

The thorax is an irregularly shaped cylinder with a narrow superior thoracic aperture (thoracic inlet) and a much larger inferior thoracic aperture closed by the diaphragm.

ApertureBoundariesClinical relevance
Superior (inlet)Manubrium · 1st ribs · T1 vertebraPancoast tumor invades brachial plexus & sympathetic chain → Horner syndrome
Inferior (outlet)Xiphoid · costal margin · ribs 11/12 · T12Closed by diaphragm; viscera "ride up" into thorax

Three compartments: R pleural cavity L pleural cavity Mediastinum (central)

Mediastinum — 4 Subdivisions HY

▾

The sternal angle (angle of Louis, T4/T5 disc) divides the mediastinum into superior and inferior; inferior is further split by the pericardium.

SubdivisionKey contents
SuperiorAortic arch + 3 branches, brachiocephalic veins, SVC origin, trachea, esophagus, thoracic duct, thymus, vagus, phrenic, L recurrent laryngeal n.
AnteriorThymus (or remnant), fat, lymph nodes, internal thoracic vessels
MiddleHeart + pericardium, roots of great vessels, main bronchi, phrenic nerves
PosteriorDescending thoracic aorta, esophagus, thoracic duct, azygos/hemiazygos, sympathetic trunk, splanchnic nerves

Sternal Angle (T4/T5) — "Anatomic Compass" HY

▾

Everything important happens here. Memorize this list — it appears on every exam.

  • Manubriosternal joint & level of rib 2 articulation (counting starts here)
  • Tracheal bifurcation into right & left main bronchi (carina)
  • Aortic arch begins and ends
  • Brachiocephalic veins unite → form SVC
  • Boundary between superior & inferior mediastinum
  • Azygos vein arches over right main bronchus
  • Ligamentum arteriosum lies just below arch
  • Cardiac plexus location
Mnemonic: "RAT BAT" — Rib 2, Aortic arch begins/ends, Tracheal bifurcation, Brachiocephalic veins unite, Azygos arches, Thoracic duct crosses midline (T4–T5).

Chest tube / thoracentesis: Insert at the upper border of the lower rib in the intercostal space to spare the NVB. Safe triangle in the axilla: lat dorsi (post) · pec major (ant) · base of axilla (sup) · 5th ICS (inf).

Intercostal Vessels & Nerves HY

▾
  • Posterior intercostal arteries: 1–2 from supreme intercostal (branch of costocervical trunk); 3–11 from descending thoracic aorta; subcostal supplies below rib 12.
  • Anterior intercostal arteries: 1–6 from internal thoracic; 7–9 from musculophrenic; 10–11 have none.
  • Veins: Drain mainly to azygos system (R) and hemiazygos/accessory hemiazygos (L).
  • Intercostal nerves (T1–T11) = anterior rami of T1–T11; T12 = subcostal. They supply skin, intercostals, and parietal pleura.
  • T4 dermatome = nipple; T10 = umbilicus; T7 = xiphoid (great landmarks!).

Breast (Mammary Gland)

▾
  • Modified sweat gland in superficial fascia overlying pec major (and partly serratus ant.).
  • Extends from rib 2 to rib 6; axillary tail of Spence pierces deep fascia at lateral edge.
  • Suspensory ligaments of Cooper — fibrous septa; involvement → skin dimpling in cancer.
  • Blood: internal thoracic (medial mammary), lateral thoracic, thoracoacromial, posterior intercostals.
  • Lymphatic drainage: ~75% to axillary nodes (esp. pectoral group); 20% to parasternal (internal thoracic); rest to opposite breast/abdominal nodes.
Breast cancer spread: Sentinel node biopsy targets the first axillary node receiving drainage. Internal mammary spread is harder to detect clinically.

Pleural Recesses

  • Costodiaphragmatic recess — lung does not fully fill on expiration; fluid collects here (best site for thoracentesis).
  • Costomediastinal recess — between costal and mediastinal pleura, anteriorly.

Hilum & Root of Lung HY

▾

Contents enter at the hilum. Arrangement of structures:

  • Right hilum (anterior → posterior): Superior pulmonary vein → Pulmonary artery → Main bronchus (eparterial bronchus to RUL is above the artery — unique to right!)
  • Left hilum: Pulmonary artery is superior (hooks over the bronchus); vein anterior; bronchus posterior.
  • Inferior pulmonary vein is the most inferior structure in both hila.
Mnemonic — RALS: Right Anterior, Left Superior (refers to pulmonary artery position relative to main bronchus).
Right bronchus: shorter, wider, more vertical → aspirated objects go right (esp. into RLL when supine, RML/RLL when upright).

Bronchopulmonary Segments

▾
  • 10 right, 8–10 left (lingular segments = LUL equivalents of RML).
  • Each segment = pyramidal unit with its own segmental bronchus + artery + vein in periphery; surgical resection unit.
  • Tertiary (segmental) bronchi follow segmental arteries. Veins lie in intersegmental planes.

Lung Blood Supply & Innervation HY

▾
  • Pulmonary arteries — carry deoxygenated blood from RV to alveoli for gas exchange.
  • Bronchial arteries — supply the lung tissue itself; usually 1 right (from 3rd posterior intercostal), 2 left (directly from aorta).
  • Pulmonary veins — 4 total (2 per lung) drain to LA with oxygenated blood.
  • Innervation: Pulmonary plexus (anterior + posterior). Parasymp (vagus) = bronchoconstrictor + secretomotor; Symp (T1–T4) = bronchodilator.
  • Lymphatic drainage: pulmonary → bronchopulmonary (hilar) → tracheobronchial → paratracheal → bronchomediastinal trunk.

Heart & Pericardium

Pericardium HY

▾
  • Outer fibrous pericardium (tough, inelastic) + inner serous pericardium (parietal + visceral/epicardial layers).
  • Innervated by phrenic nerve (C3–C5) → pericarditis pain referred to neck/shoulder.
  • Blood: pericardiacophrenic artery (branch of internal thoracic).

Pericardial Sinuses

  • Transverse sinus — between great arteries (aorta + pulm trunk) anteriorly and great veins (SVC + pulm veins) posteriorly. Surgical use: cross-clamp during CABG.
  • Oblique sinus — behind LA, bounded by pulmonary veins & IVC; cul-de-sac.
Cardiac tamponade: Fluid in pericardial sac → impaired filling → Beck's triad (hypotension, distended neck veins, muffled heart sounds). Treat with pericardiocentesis (subxiphoid approach).

Chamber landmarks

  • RA: Crista terminalis separates smooth (sinus venarum) from rough (pectinate); fossa ovalis on interatrial septum (closed foramen ovale).
  • RV: Trabeculae carneae, septomarginal trabecula (moderator band) carries R bundle branch, conus arteriosus → pulm trunk.
  • LA: Smooth (from pulmonary veins); auricle has pectinate.
  • LV: Thickest wall (~3× RV), trabeculae carneae, 2 papillary muscles (ant + post).

Coronary veins

  • Most drain via coronary sinus (in coronary sulcus posteriorly) → RA.
  • Tributaries: great cardiac v. (with LAD) · middle cardiac v. (with PDA) · small cardiac v. · oblique vein of LA (of Marshall).
  • Anterior cardiac veins drain directly to RA.
  • Smallest cardiac veins (Thebesian) drain directly into chambers.
MI localization: LAD occlusion → anteroseptal MI (V1–V4). RCA → inferior MI (II, III, aVF) + may cause bradyarrhythmia (AV node). LCX → lateral MI (I, aVL, V5–V6).

Conduction System HY

▾

SA node (RA, near SVC opening) → atrial contraction → AV node (interatrial septum, near coronary sinus) → Bundle of His → R + L bundle branches in IV septum → Purkinje fibers.

  • R bundle branch travels via moderator band → anterior papillary muscle of RV.
  • SA node fires ~70 bpm; AV node ~50 bpm; ventricle ~30 bpm.
  • Innervation: Symp (T1–T4) ↑ rate; Parasymp (vagus) ↓ rate.

Mediastinum — Key Structures

Great Vessels HY

▾
  • Aortic arch — 3 branches L→R: brachiocephalic (→ R subclavian + R common carotid) · L common carotid · L subclavian.
  • Ligamentum arteriosum connects arch to L pulmonary artery (remnant of ductus arteriosus). L recurrent laryngeal n. hooks under it.
  • SVC — formed by union of R + L brachiocephalic veins at right 1st costal cartilage; receives azygos before entering RA.
  • IVC — pierces diaphragm at T8, immediately enters RA.
  • Pulmonary trunk — bifurcates at T4–T5, ligamentum arteriosum at bifurcation.

Esophagus HY

▾
  • ~25 cm long; cricoid (C6) → cardia of stomach (T11).
  • 3 constrictions: cricopharyngeus (C6) · arch of aorta + L main bronchus (T4) · diaphragmatic hiatus (T10).
  • Pierces diaphragm at T10 with vagal trunks (anterior = L vagus, posterior = R vagus).
  • Blood: inferior thyroid, esophageal branches of aorta, L gastric (porto-systemic anastomosis).
Portal hypertension → esophageal varices at lower 1/3 (L gastric vein anastomosis). Foreign bodies tend to lodge at the three constrictions.

Trachea HY

▾
  • C6 (cricoid) → T4/T5 (carina). 16–20 C-shaped hyaline cartilages (open posteriorly — trachealis muscle).
  • Bifurcates into R + L main bronchi at carina. R main bronchus: shorter, wider, more vertical.
  • Innervated by recurrent laryngeal nn. (motor + sensory below cords) & vagus.

Azygos System HY

▾

Drains posterior thorax, abdomen wall, and back; provides collateral channel between SVC and IVC.

  • Azygos (R side) — ascends along R of vertebral bodies; arches over R main bronchus at T4 → SVC.
  • Hemiazygos (lower L) — crosses to R at ~T9 to join azygos.
  • Accessory hemiazygos (upper L) — crosses to R at ~T8.
IVC obstruction: Blood reroutes through azygos system → engorgement on imaging.

Thoracic Duct HY

▾
  • Originates at cisterna chyli (L1/L2) → passes through aortic hiatus (T12) with aorta and azygos.
  • Ascends in posterior mediastinum to the right of midline, then crosses to left at T4/T5.
  • Empties into junction of L subclavian + L internal jugular veins.
  • Drains all lymph below diaphragm + L side of body above (3/4 of body). Right lymphatic duct drains R upper quadrant.
Chylothorax: Injury → milky lymph in pleural space. Left-side injury (above T4/T5) = L chylothorax; right-side injury (below T4/T5) = R chylothorax.

Diaphragm

Structure & Attachments HY

▾
  • Musculotendinous dome; central tendon + muscular periphery.
  • Three attachments: sternal (xiphoid) · costal (ribs 7–12) · lumbar (crura + arcuate ligaments).
  • R crus arises from L1–L3; L crus from L1–L2.

Innervation & Blood Supply HY

▾
  • Motor: phrenic nerve (C3–C5) — "C3, 4, 5 keeps the diaphragm alive". Each phrenic supplies its own half.
  • Sensory: phrenic (central) + lower intercostals (peripheral).
  • Blood: pericardiacophrenic, musculophrenic, superior/inferior phrenic arteries.
Diaphragmatic irritation (e.g., subphrenic abscess, blood) → referred shoulder pain (C3–C5 dermatomes = supraclavicular).

Clinical Correlations — High-Yield Boards Relevance

Pneumothorax & Pleural Effusion HY

▾
Pneumothorax: Air in pleural space → lung collapse (negative pressure lost). Tension pneumothorax → mediastinal shift away from affected side → reduced venous return. Decompress with needle at 2nd ICS, midclavicular line, then chest tube.
Pleural effusion: Fluid (transudate vs. exudate by Light's criteria). Collects in costodiaphragmatic recess. Thoracentesis at posterior axillary line, ICS 7–9, above the rib.
Hemothorax — usually from torn intercostal artery; check posterior intercostals (from descending aorta).

Heart Sounds & Murmurs HY

▾
  • Aortic stenosis: Crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur at R 2nd ICS, radiates to carotids.
  • Mitral regurg: Holosystolic murmur at apex, radiates to L axilla.
  • Mitral stenosis: Mid-diastolic rumble with opening snap at apex (best in L lateral decubitus).
  • Aortic regurg: Decrescendo diastolic at L sternal border.
  • S3 = volume overload (CHF, MR); S4 = stiff ventricle (HTN, AS).

Aortic Dissection & Aneurysm HY

▾
  • Type A (Stanford) — involves ascending aorta → surgical emergency.
  • Type B — descending aorta only → usually medical mgmt.
  • Sudden "tearing" chest/back pain radiating between scapulae.
  • Aneurysm of arch → may compress L recurrent laryngeal n. → hoarseness.

Rib Fractures & Flail Chest HY

▾
  • Middle ribs (5–9) most commonly fractured.
  • Rib 1 fx — high energy, may injure subclavian vessels & brachial plexus.
  • Ribs 9–11 fx — may injure spleen (left) or liver (right).
  • Flail chest — 3+ consecutive ribs broken in 2+ places → paradoxical motion (segment moves IN on inspiration).

Common Imaging Relevance HY

▾
  • Counting ribs on CXR: start at sternal angle (rib 2).
  • Cardiothoracic ratio > 0.5 = cardiomegaly (PA view).
  • "Boot-shaped heart" = Tetralogy of Fallot.
  • "Egg on a string" = transposition of great arteries.
  • Widened mediastinum > 8 cm on supine CXR → suspect aortic dissection/rupture.
  • Kerley B lines + perihilar haziness → pulmonary edema (CHF).

▾
R main bronchus = Shorter, Wider, more Vertical → "SWV" — foreign bodies go right!

Coronary Dominance

▾
Dominance = which artery gives the PDA.
~70% right dominant (RCA → PDA) · ~10% left dominant (LCX → PDA) · ~20% co-dominant.

SA / AV Node Locations

▾
SA node: RA, near SVC opening (sulcus terminalis).
AV node: Interatrial septum, near coronary sinus opening.
Both usually supplied by RCA → inferior MI → bradyarrhythmia.
